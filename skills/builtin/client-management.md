# Client Management

## Project Scoping
- Always define what's IN scope AND what's OUT of scope in writing
- Break projects into milestones with clear deliverables per milestone
- Set realistic timelines — add 20-30% buffer for unknowns
- Get sign-off on scope before starting work

## Proposals & Quotes
- Include: problem statement, proposed solution, deliverables, timeline, price, payment terms
- Be specific about what you'll deliver — vague proposals lead to scope creep
- Include a clause for changes outside the original scope (hourly rate for extras)

## Communication
- Respond to client messages within 24 hours on business days
- Send weekly status updates on active projects (even if nothing changed)
- Document decisions made in meetings via email ("Just to confirm what we agreed...")
- When delivering work, explain what you did and why

## Billing
- Invoice promptly — don't let invoices pile up
- Use NET 15 or NET 30 payment terms
- Follow up on overdue invoices at 3 days, 7 days, 14 days after due
- Keep records of all time spent and what was done

## Difficult Situations
- Scope creep: "That's outside our original scope. I can do that for an additional X."
- Late payment: be polite but firm; pause work if payment is significantly overdue
- Unhappy client: listen first, understand the issue, propose a concrete solution
- Scope disputes: refer back to the signed proposal/contract

## Red Flags (Before Taking a Client)
- No clear budget or "we'll figure it out"
- Unrealistic deadlines with no flexibility
- Dismissive of your process or previous work
- Wants to start without a contract
